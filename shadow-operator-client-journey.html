The System
Four Calls.
One Complete Partnership.

Every client engagement follows the same four-call structure. Not four random meetings — a deliberate sequence where each call builds on the last and moves the partnership forward.

01
Strategy Call
Day 1. Onboard the creator, walk through the system, sign the agreement, launch the market research.
02
Offer Design Call
Week 1–2. Data is in. Choose the right offer model and lock the build timeline.
03
Pre-Launch Call
Days before launch. Polish scripts, align on content sequence, confirm the launch plan.
04
Debrief Call
Post-launch. Review what worked, propose the next phase, convert the project into a partnership.

Call 01 · Day 1
Strategy Call

Set the foundation. Align on the system. Get commitment before execution starts.

◆
Walk through the 4-phase monetisation system — no surprises later
◆
Explain the lead magnet approach — why market data comes before offer design
◆
Set up their Whop page together or assign as first action
◆
Define both roles clearly — what you build, what they do
◆
Sign the partnership agreement
Output: signed agreement · lead magnet live · market research begins

Call 02 · Week 1–2
Offer Design Call

Data is in. Now you decide what gets built first — community or high-ticket — based on what the audience actually showed you.

◆
Present ICP findings from the lead magnet responses
◆
Recommend community or high-ticket — explain the reasoning, not just the choice
◆
Walk through the offer framework — structure, pricing, delivery model
◆
Agree on product creation + launch timeline (~1 week build)

Prepare your ICP analysis before this call — not during. The recommendation should be clear and confident, backed by data.

Output: offer direction locked · build timeline agreed
Shadow Operator
04 / 07
Call 03 · Days Before Launch
Pre-Launch Call

The system is built. This call makes sure the creator is ready — confident, clear, and aligned on every touchpoint before it goes live.

◆
Review and polish the marketing scripts and VSL
◆
Walk through the story and content sequence — what gets posted, when, why
◆
Confirm launch timeline and each person's role during it
◆
Handle any last-minute objections or adjustments

Most operators skip this call. Having it sets you apart — creators go into launch with confidence instead of anxiety.

Output: creator confident · launch sequence approved · go live
Shadow Operator
05 / 07
Call 04 · Post-Launch
Debrief Call

The most important call for retention. This is where you show the operator mindset — honest, data-driven, already thinking about what comes next.

◆
Review what worked and what didn't — revenue, conversions, drop-offs
◆
Identify offer improvements — pricing, positioning, delivery
◆
Propose the next launch or evergreen system
◆
Re-frame the relationship — from project to long-term partnership

Always end this call with a clear next step proposed by you. Don't wait for the creator to ask what's next.

Output: next phase scoped · retainer or repeat project agreed

What Makes This Work
Key Notes on the System
✓
Data before offer design is the right call Most operators skip the market research phase and build based on assumption. Your lead magnet approach is a genuine differentiator.
✓
Four calls is the right number Enough structure to feel professional without over-engineering the relationship. Each call has a clear purpose and output.
✓
The pre-launch call is your edge Most operators skip Call 3. Having it means creators go into launch confident — not anxious. That alone improves results.
!
Call 4 needs a retention pitch baked in Don't just debrief — always close with "here's what I recommend we do next." Leaving without a next step agreed risks losing the client.
!
The build week is your biggest retention risk Between Call 2 and Call 3, creators see nothing happening. Weekly updates in the app solve this — silence kills trust.
